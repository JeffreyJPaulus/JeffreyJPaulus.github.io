About

My first experience's with coding hacking at Quick Basic on my first "laptop" which was old even then... an Amstrad PPC512D followed by a Mac Pascal class in Highschool. After my preliminary programming experiences, I decided to pursue a degree not in programming but the hardware running the programs which included a semester learning assembly code for a Motorola processor providing great insights into "firmware".

After Graduating with an AAS degree in electronics and working in various hardware related roles, I added a BS Management degree to my resume which enabled me to work with people at all levels of Micron. As my current job evolves, I am finding more of a need for software development skills as most of the hardware I support is now overseas. While some of my past experiences have allowed me to get this far, I am finding gaps in my understanding and knowledge.

This triggered my next step in my continuous learning journey at CWI where I am pursuing an AAS Degree in Software Development. The classes currently coming to an end this first semester have taught me HTML5\CSS web design as well as C# which was not only an introduction the language but also helped me grasp the concept of Object Oriented Programming. I believe this alone will enable me to develop what I am currently tasked to do at work as well as enable me to fill more needed roles in the future. I am looking forward to my next classes learning additional lessons in other languages and new concepts, I am excited to continue this path towards the degree.
