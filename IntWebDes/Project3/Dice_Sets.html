DnD Dice World FAQs

What are DnD Dice?

DnD is a role playing game which uses dice as a random number generator to determine outcomes of decisions made by the players as well as guide the "Dungeon Master" in determine interactions the group may encounter.

Why all of these sides and how to use them?

The polyhedral dice are a collection of different numbered surfaces d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, and d100 sided dice (although most users prefer to combine two 10's, one being the 10 place and the other being the ones to make a combination of 0-100 possible results.)

Dice Jail?

The term Dice Jail is used for a place to put dice that have been providing poor results for the user. This "Time out" is believed to help convince the dice to perform better the next use.

What are Polyhedral Dice?

Polyheral: defined as "of pertaining to, or having the shape of a polyhedron which is a 3D object with multiple flat surfaces." from the colins dictionary.

Resin vs Acrylic?

Resin dice are typically tumbled to soften their edges. Acrylic dice are injection molded, then covered all over in paint and then they go through a tumbling process to removes the paint on the outside surfaces and to polish them. Acrylic are the easiest to mass produce, and therefore the lowest priced.
